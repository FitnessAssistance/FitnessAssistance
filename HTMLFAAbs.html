Abdominal Muscles

One of the most widely known muscles are the abs/abdominal muscles and they play an important role in our everyday lives. Doing core workouts is an integral aspect of a well-rounded wellness plan.

What is Abdominal Strength?

  • The strength of the muscles in the abdomen
  • Enhances balance and stability
  • Includes muscles in the back and pelvis
  • Core muscles keep your body stable and upright
  • Used to maintain proper posture
  • Without good abdominal strength, you will have difficulty performing daily activities

What Happens With Weak Abs?

  • Poor posture
  • Trouble breathing
  • Difficulty performing daily activities
  • Lower back pain
  • Slower metabolism

The Abdominal Muscles Functions & Facts:

  • The abdominal muscles are also called core muscles
  • They control our sneezing, breathing, speaking, and ability to keep good posture
  • The abdominal muscles responsible for turning your spine are the internal obliques located at your sides
  • Erector spinae are muscles in charge of your posture
  • Strong abdominal muscles helps increase your metabolism letting you keep a flat stomach
